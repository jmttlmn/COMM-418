## [An immigration court few have heard of is quietly shaping policy behind the scenes](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/20/nx-s1-5707535/trump-immigration-detention-appeals-board-deportation))
### NPR

This is a good story that uses publicly available data to find a pattern and creates a very interesting story out of that pattern. The three data visualizations used are very good and work well in the article. If I were working on this article, some reporting on how the board operated before the Obama era (if it existed) might've been interesting to put what is happening in context. Another point that I found interesting was the use of AI in the reporting. At the bottom of the article, there is a disclaimer saying that AI was used to go through the cases from 2009-2026 to determine which side was favored in a given decision. It was an interesting choice, especially since they said NPR reporters and an independent third party verified the results. I am not necessarily opposed to using AI to go through datasets to assist with a larger reporting goal. However, if we're still at the point where AI work needs such a high level of scrutiny to verify its accuracy, it makes me wonder if this actually benefited the reporters on the story.
