## [The global chokepoint in the Strait of Hormuz](https://www.reuters.com/graphics/IRAN-CRISIS/MAPS/znpnmelervl/#the-global-chokepoint-in-the-strait-of-hormuz)
### Reuters

They did a strong job using simple maps and charts to show why the Strait of Hormuz matters, especially by pairing a clear oil flow figure with an oil price time series so you can see scale and market reaction quickly. I also liked that the visuals are broken into digestible sections, which makes it easy to scan and understand the geography and risk factors. What I felt like it’s missing is a more direct, up-to-date visualization of the current shipping slowdown, like a before-and-after count of vessels transiting the strait over the last week. Since the story also mentions LNG reliance, a chart showing LNG volumes or share through the strait would help quantify that risk beyond oil.
