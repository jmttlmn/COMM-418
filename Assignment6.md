#### This stacked bar graph shows the total number of licensed pharmacies located in D.C., grouped by ward. As we can see, the far east Wards 5, 6, 7 and 8 dominate the totals, accounting for 416 of the 563 pharmacies located in the district. According to 2022 census data from [DC.gov](https://planning.dc.gov/maps-wards-2022), all wards have a relatively similar population, with the highest being Ward 5 with 89,617 residents and the lowest being Ward 6 with 84,266 residents. This is particularly interesting because Ward 6 has the highest number of pharmacies, despite having the lowest population and being one of the smaller wards by area. That suggests pharmacy locations are not only responding to where people live, but also to where people work, shop and pass through. One likely factor is that Ward 6 includes a large share of central-city commercial and retail activity, along with major transit corridors, which could create high demand for pharmacies from commuters, office workers and visitors in addition to local residents. Ward 1, being at the bottom of this graph, checks out as well, since it is the smallest by area, which naturally limits how many retail sites can fit within its boundaries. It is also possible that ward-level totals reflect differences in zoning patterns and the concentration of large pharmacy chains versus independent storefronts. The more northern wards, particularly Wards 3 and 4, are made up of primarily residential neighborhoods, which explains why they are at the bottom despite being large by area. This dataset was located on [Open Data DC](https://opendata.dc.gov/datasets/DCGIS::pharmacy-locations/about) and was collected from the Health Regulation and Licensing Administration’s Pharmacy Control Division within DC Health. Pharmacy licenses are updated monthly through an automated process according to Open Data DC.
